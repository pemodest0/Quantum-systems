# Paper Card: Maier 2019

## Source

Maier, C.; Brydges, T.; Jurcevic, P.; Trautmann, N.; Hempel, C.; Lanyon, B. P.; Hauke, P.; Blatt, R.; Roos, C. F. "Environment-Assisted Quantum Transport in a 10-qubit Network." Physical Review Letters 122, 050501 (2019). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.122.050501

## Physical question

Can environment-assisted quantum transport be observed in a controlled quantum simulator?

## Model

Trapped-ion spin network with engineered static disorder and temporally varying dephasing noise. Electronic excitation transfer between ions represents transport.

## Central equations

The experiment realizes an interacting spin model with controlled disorder and dephasing. The conceptual scan is over disorder and noise strength.

## Observables

- excitation transfer.
- disorder-dependent localization.
- dephasing-assisted window.
- high-noise suppression.

## Main result

The experiment observes a crossover from coherent dynamics/localization to environment-assisted transport and then to suppression at strong noise.

## Limitations

This is a controlled simulator, not a direct simulation of every material. Its parameters must not be copied blindly into unrelated media.

## Relation with our lab

This is the strongest experimental guardrail for the pattern we seek: low-noise limitation, intermediate assistance, and high-noise suppression.
