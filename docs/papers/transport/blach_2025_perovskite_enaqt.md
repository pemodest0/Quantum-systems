# Blach Et Al. 2025: Perovskite ENAQT

## Source

Daria D. Blach et al., `Environment-assisted quantum transport of excitons in perovskite nanocrystal superlattices`, Nature Communications 16, 1270 (2025). DOI: `10.1038/s41467-024-55812-8`.

## Physical Question

Can environment-assisted quantum transport be observed in a modern solid-state excitonic material?

## Model / Experiment

Ultrafast imaging of exciton propagation in perovskite nanocrystal superlattices, interpreted with a stochastic Anderson-localization model.

## Central Claim

Transport can improve when static disorder and dephasing are balanced. The material experiment connects coherent propagation, disorder/localization, and environment-assisted transport.

## Limitations

This is a material experiment. Our current model does not include nanocrystal structure, exciton parameters, temperature dependence, or the experimental imaging pipeline.

## Relation with our lab

This is modern experimental motivation. Our current graph model can reproduce the qualitative idea of a balanced regime, but it cannot reproduce perovskite nanocrystal parameters.
