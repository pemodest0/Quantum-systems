# Paper Card: Razzoli 2021

## Source

Razzoli, L.; Paris, M. G. A.; Bordone, P. "Transport Efficiency of Continuous-Time Quantum Walks on Graphs." Entropy 23, 85 (2021). DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/e23010085

## Physical question

How do graph topology, initial state, and trap placement affect quantum-walk transport efficiency?

## Model

Continuous-time quantum walks on graphs with a trap vertex. The focus is coherent transport and graph-dependent efficiency.

## Central equations

```text
H from graph structure
eta(t) from trapping probability
```

## Observables

- transport efficiency.
- role of initial state.
- trap-dependent subspaces.
- graph symmetry and regularity.

## Main result

Transport efficiency depends strongly on graph structure, initial state, and trap placement. Connectivity alone is a poor predictor.

## Limitations

The paper is mainly coherent/trap-focused and does not replace a full noise-and-disorder scan.

## Relation with our lab

This directly supports treating target placement as a first-order variable in ring and graph campaigns.
