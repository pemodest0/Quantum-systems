# Paper Card: Anderson 1958

## Source

Anderson, P. W. "Absence of Diffusion in Certain Random Lattices." Physical Review 109, 1492-1505 (1958). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRev.109.1492

## Physical question

Can randomness in a lattice prevent diffusion?

## Model

Quantum hopping in a random lattice with site-to-site energy variation.

## Central equations

The core idea is a disordered tight-binding-like setting where random local energies can suppress extended transport.

## Observables

- diffusion or absence of diffusion.
- localization due to disorder.

## Main result

Strong enough disorder can localize states and prevent ordinary diffusion.

## Limitations

This is not an open-system Lindblad transport paper. It is a foundational disorder/localization reference.

## Relation with our lab

Use it to justify why increasing `W/J` can reduce target arrival and why disorder ensembles matter.
