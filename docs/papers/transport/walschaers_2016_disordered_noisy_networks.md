# Paper Card: Walschaers 2016

## Source

Walschaers, M.; Schlawin, F.; Wellens, T.; Buchleitner, A. "Quantum Transport on Disordered and Noisy Networks: An Interplay of Structural Complexity and Uncertainty." Annual Review of Condensed Matter Physics 7, 223-248 (2016). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-conmatphys-031115-011327

## Physical question

How do disorder, noise, finite size, and network complexity combine in quantum transport?

## Model

Review of finite, disordered, noisy networks across settings including photosynthetic complexes and photonic systems.

## Central equations

The review compares coherent, disordered, and noisy transport descriptions rather than one single equation.

## Observables

- transport efficiency.
- localization and delocalization.
- finite-size behavior.
- network complexity effects.

## Main result

Finite networks can show behavior that is not captured by simple asymptotic limits. Structure, uncertainty, and noise must be analyzed together.

## Limitations

As a review, it is a conceptual guardrail. Specific claims still require model-level simulations and parameter sweeps.

## Relation with our lab

Use it to justify our campaign style: small controlled media, finite-size checks, disorder ensembles, and no overclaiming from one graph.
