# Paper Card: Muelken and Blumen 2011

## Source

Mülken, O.; Blumen, A. "Continuous-time quantum walks: Models for coherent transport on complex networks." Physics Reports 502, 37-87 (2011). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physrep.2011.01.002

## Physical question

How do continuous-time quantum walks model coherent transport on networks?

## Model

Continuous-time quantum walk on graphs, often with Hamiltonians related to adjacency or Laplacian matrices. The review also discusses trapping and disorder.

## Central equations

```text
|psi(t)> = exp(-i H t)|psi(0)>
p_i(t) = |<i|psi(t)>|^2
```

For open or trapped cases, effective non-Hermitian or master-equation extensions may be used.

## Observables

- site occupation probabilities.
- survival probability.
- transport efficiency with traps.
- dependence on graph topology.

## Main result

Graph structure strongly shapes coherent transport. Quantum walks can differ sharply from classical random walks because of interference.

## Limitations

Pure CTQW does not by itself include environmental dephasing, loss, or realistic material baths.

## Relation with our lab

Use it as the baseline for graph topology and coherent-limit expectations.
