# Gamble Et Al. 2010: Two-Particle Quantum Walks And Graph Isomorphism

## Source

John King Gamble, Mark Friesen, Dong Zhou, Robert Joynt, S. N. Coppersmith, `Two-particle quantum walks applied to the graph isomorphism problem`, Physical Review A 81, 052313 (2010). DOI: `10.1103/PhysRevA.81.052313`.

## Physical Question

Can quantum-walk dynamics distinguish graph structures that are hard for simpler invariants?

## Model

Single-particle and two-particle quantum walks on highly symmetric graphs.

## Central Claim

Interacting two-particle walks can distinguish some graph structures that noninteracting or single-particle approaches cannot.

## Limitations

This paper uses two-particle quantum walks. Our current transport model is single-excitation and should not claim the same graph-isomorphism power.

## Relation with our lab

This is a limitation guardrail. Our present model is single-excitation transport, so it should not claim to solve graph isomorphism or exhaust all graph-distinguishability information.
