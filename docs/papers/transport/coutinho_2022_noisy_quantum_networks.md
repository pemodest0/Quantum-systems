# Paper Card: Coutinho 2022

## Source

Coutinho, B. C.; Munro, W. J.; Nemoto, K.; Omar, Y. et al. "Robustness of noisy quantum networks." Communications Physics 5, 105 (2022). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s42005-022-00866-7

## Physical question

How robust are quantum networks when links and nodes fail or become noisy?

## Model

Quantum complex networks with noisy repeater-like nodes and random loss of links/nodes. This is not the same model as our single-excitation transport lab, but it gives useful network-robustness guardrails.

## Central equations

The paper uses network robustness and connectivity analysis rather than our exact Lindblad target-arrival model.

## Observables

- network connectivity.
- robustness under link and node failures.
- phase-transition-like loss of network function.

## Main result

Noisy quantum networks can show abrupt robustness failures as links or nodes fail.

## Limitations

Do not use this paper as proof of dephasing-assisted target arrival. It is a network robustness reference, not a direct ENAQT model.

## Relation with our lab

Use it when discussing robustness, graph failure, and why mean performance alone is not enough.
