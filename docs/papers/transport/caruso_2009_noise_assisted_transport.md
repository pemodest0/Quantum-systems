# Paper Card: Caruso 2009

## Source

Caruso, F.; Chin, A. W.; Datta, A.; Huelga, S. F.; Plenio, M. B. "Highly efficient energy excitation transfer in light-harvesting complexes: the fundamental role of noise-assisted transport." Journal of Chemical Physics 131, 105106 (2009). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1063/1.3223548

## Physical question

Which mechanisms allow noise to improve excitation transfer in light-harvesting-inspired networks?

## Model

Dissipative quantum network with coherent couplings, noise/dephasing mechanisms, and transfer channels.

## Central equations

```text
d rho / dt = -i[H, rho] + noise terms + trapping/loss terms
```

## Observables

- transfer efficiency.
- trapping subspaces.
- effect of dephasing and line broadening.

## Main result

Noise can help by reducing destructive interference and by opening effective transfer pathways.

## Limitations

The mechanisms are model-specific. The result does not imply that every noisy system transports better.

## Relation with our lab

Use it when explaining why an unfavorable target in a symmetric graph may improve under moderate phase scrambling.
