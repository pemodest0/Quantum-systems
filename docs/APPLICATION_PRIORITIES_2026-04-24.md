## Decision

Use the transport project as the MSc core:

**Coherent and dissipative transport in simple open quantum networks.**

Short framing:

> The project studies how topology, disorder, dephasing and loss control
> excitation transport efficiency in finite open quantum networks. The goal is
> to build a reproducible numerical laboratory, identify transport regimes, and
> connect the results to experimentally relevant open-system platforms.

This is stronger for the application than trying to present every simulation
already generated. The committee needs a focused research question, not a folder
dump.

## 24-Month MSc Structure

Months 1--3:

- literature review on open quantum systems, Lindblad dynamics, transport and ENAQT;
- clean validation of the simulation layer;
- define benchmark graph families and observables.

Months 4--8:

- topology, disorder, sink and dephasing scans;
- identify coherent, localized, environment-assisted and lossy regimes;
- produce reproducible baseline reports.

Months 9--14:

- parameter-space classification;
- robustness studies and model comparisons;
- connect signatures to experimentally accessible observables.

Months 15--20:

- refine theoretical interpretation;
- prepare article-style results and thesis chapters;
- compare with relevant literature and possible experimental platforms.

Months 21--24:

- consolidate thesis, figures and reproducibility package;
- final defense preparation;
- paper/preprint draft if results are mature.

## Possible FAPESP Doctoral Continuation

A doctoral project can extend the MSc in three directions:

- non-Markovian transport and process-tensor diagnostics;
- control of transport through engineered noise and graph modulation;
- experimental validation in NMR, photonic or superconducting-like platforms.

The important point is to keep the MSc scoped enough to finish, while leaving a
clear path to a larger FAPESP-style doctoral program.
